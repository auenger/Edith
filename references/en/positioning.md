# Positioning

## What JARVIS is

JARVIS is an **agent-first organizational capability layer**.

It helps agents:
- understand company digital assets,
- route toward the right repos and execution surfaces,
- follow real workflows,
- and preserve what they learn so the organization compounds capability over time.

A JARVIS rollout usually includes:
- memory and routing,
- source-facing skills,
- repo-local skills,
- workflow skills,
- rollout ownership,
- and update discipline.

## What JARVIS is not

JARVIS is not:
- a raw document mirror,
- an issue dump,
- a one-shot repo generator,
- a replacement for repo-local source of truth,
- or a promise that one expert can finish everything in one pass.

## The real unit of value

The real unit of value is not “a generated repo”.

The real unit of value is a **working closed loop** where an agent can:
1. find the right company context,
2. enter the right repo or system,
3. help complete real work,
4. and write back durable learnings.

If the rollout cannot unlock such a loop, it is still scaffolding.

## Why this matters

Without JARVIS, agents stay session-local and amnesiac.

With JARVIS, organizations can start to:
- reduce cross-team waiting cost,
- preserve engineering judgment,
- help more roles participate in broader delivery loops,
- and gradually move toward One Person Team operating patterns.

## Working stance

Treat JARVIS construction as:
- organizational design,
- knowledge architecture,
- and workflow enablement.

Do not treat it as a documentation beautification exercise.
