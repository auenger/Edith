# Rollout and Ownership

Treat EDITH as a multi-owner rollout, not a one-time construction task.

## Why ownership matters

EDITH usually spans:
- different digital assets,
- different repos,
- different teams,
- different approval boundaries,
- and different kinds of source of truth.

One agent or one human may start the work, but durable rollout requires explicit ownership.

## Suggested rollout stages

### Stage 0 — Clarify
- define the business intent
- choose the first high-value loop
- identify initial owners

### Stage 1 — Pilot
- inventory only the sources, repos, and workflows needed for the first loop
- create the first backlog
- generate the first-pass scaffolds

### Stage 2 — Operationalize
- add missing source / repo / workflow skills for the pilot area
- establish writeback rules
- make handoff between owners reliable

### Stage 3 — Scale
- expand coverage to adjacent workflows
- improve governance and quality checks
- reduce re-discovery cost across teams

## Owner types to capture

At minimum, identify:
- business owner
- knowledge owner
- repo owner(s)
- workflow owner(s)
- EDITH maintainer / steward

## Good rollout output

A good rollout makes it easy to answer:
- who continues this next,
- what they should build next,
- what is blocked,
- what is already usable,
- and what maturity level the current EDITH has reached.

## Failure mode to avoid

Do not present early-stage scaffolding as if the organization already has full EDITH coverage.
